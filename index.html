The challenge

Sourcing from Pakistan without someone on the ground is high-risk.

You can't walk the factory floor from abroad. Suppliers overstate capacity, understate lead times, and sometimes deliver production that bears no resemblance to the samples they sent.

Journey Trades operates as your eyes and hands in Pakistan — visiting facilities, verifying documents, coordinating samples, and escalating problems before they become expensive mistakes.

What we do

Six services. One team in Pakistan.

01

Supplier Discovery

We identify and shortlist manufacturers that match your product, quality, and capacity requirements — drawing on our network and independent research.

02

Factory Verification

We visit, document, and assess every supplier before you're introduced to them. You receive a written report with photographs and our assessment.

03

Sample Coordination

We manage the full sample cycle: briefing the factory, tracking timelines, reviewing quality on arrival, and shipping to you with a written assessment.

04

Quality Inspections

We inspect production at key stages and report directly to you — with photographic evidence and a clear pass/fail assessment against your specification.

05

Communication Management

We handle all day-to-day communication with your Pakistan suppliers — chasing, escalating, and translating operational details into clear updates for you.

06

Custom Sourcing Projects

We take on complex sourcing briefs that require market research, category mapping, or multi-supplier coordination — scoped and priced before any commitment is made.

Due diligence

What we check before recommending a supplier.

Every supplier we recommend has been through a structured on-the-ground assessment. We don't shortlist factories on reputation or directory listings alone.

01

Initial Capability Review

We assess production capacity, minimum orders, and product range before scheduling any on-site visit.

02

Document & Registration Check

We verify business registration, trade memberships, and any certifications the factory holds or claims.

03

On-Site Factory Visit

We visit the facility in person — photographing equipment, production areas, worker conditions, and storage.

04

Sample & Quality Evaluation

We review samples against your brief before forwarding them, flagging any quality or specification issues at source.

05

Reference Check

Where possible, we speak with other buyers who have worked with the supplier to validate their track record.
